Kiran K. Kethineni

Ph.D. Candidate | Architecting Adaptable AI for the Industrial Edge

I engineer intelligence that survives the real world.
Currently a Ph.D. candidate at the Smart Electronic Systems Laboratory (SESL), University of North Texas, my research tackles the rigidity of modern AI. I move beyond fragile, task-specific models to build adaptable, domain-aware systems that thrive in dynamic environments—from crop fields to factory floors.

My work proves that powerful AI doesn't require massive resources. I have pioneered memory-efficient architectures (Chroma-Sense, SOIL) that bring deep learning to microcontrollers, and developed zero-shot adaptation techniques (WeedOut, XpressWeed) that allow machines to master new tasks in minutes, not months. By integrating robotic path planning (SprayCraft) with interpretable reasoning (Semantic-Search), I build systems that are not just autonomous, but transparent and collaborative.

Graduating in May 2026, I am seeking to translate these innovations into the automation industry—spanning mining, agriculture, and industrial robotics. My mission is to build technology that handles the "dirty work": simplifying complexity, ensuring safety in hazardous environments, and lifting the standard of work for human operators. I build the bridge where complex data meets simple, safe action.
